Who We Are

Rooted in place.
Growing together.

Canopy Commons is a Travis County nonprofit on a mission to bring native trees to every available acre of public land — from neighborhood parks and creek corridors to school campuses and highway medians.

"Every tree we plant is a promise to future Austinites — shade, clean air, and a living piece of Texas."

We work closely with Travis County Parks, the City of Austin Urban Forestry program, and community volunteers to identify, plant, and steward native species suited to Central Texas soils and climate.

Our Programs

How we're building
the canopy

Public Land Planting

We partner with Travis County Parks, Austin Parks & Recreation, and AISD to identify suitable sites and plant native trees on public land — parks, greenbelts, creek banks, and school grounds.

Community Volunteer Events

Monthly planting days bring together neighbors, students, and businesses to dig in — literally. No experience needed. We provide tools, trees, training, and the satisfaction of leaving something lasting.

Stewardship & Care

Planting is just the start. Our stewardship crews return to mulch, water, and monitor every tree we plant — ensuring survival through Austin's brutal summers and drought cycles.

Native Species Focus

Every tree we plant is native to Central Texas — live oak, cedar elm, Texas redbud, bald cypress, and more. Native trees support pollinators, wildlife, and withstand local climate extremes with minimal irrigation.

Equity-First Siting

We prioritize neighborhoods with the least tree canopy — often lower-income communities in East and Southeast Austin — to fight the urban heat island effect where it hits hardest.

Education & Advocacy

From school workshops on Central Texas ecology to testifying for stronger urban forestry policy, we build the knowledge and political will to keep Travis County's canopy growing for decades.

Get Involved

Ready to put down roots?

    Sign Up to Volunteer

    Join one of our monthly planting events. No experience required — just wear closed-toed shoes and bring water.

  • 02

    Donate to the Canopy Fund

    Every $25 funds one native tree, planted and cared for on public land in Travis County. 100% goes to program costs.

  • 03

    Nominate a Site

    Know a sunny patch of park, a bare schoolyard, or a stripped creek bank? Nominate it and we'll assess it for planting.

    Businesses, schools, HOAs, and government agencies — reach out about corporate planting days, sponsorships, and site agreements.
